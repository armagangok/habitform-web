Incorporating robust statistical features into a habit tracker application is essential to enhance user engagement and satisfaction. Based on user preferences and successful implementations in existing apps, here are key statistical features that users value:

1. Progress Visualization

Users appreciate visual representations of their habit development. Effective methods include:
	•	Streaks: Highlighting consecutive days of habit completion motivates users to maintain consistency.  ￼
	•	Charts and Graphs: Displaying data through monthly mood line graphs or average daily mood bar charts helps users understand trends and patterns.  ￼

2. Detailed Analytics

Providing in-depth insights into habit performance enables users to make informed decisions. Features such as:
	•	Completion Rates: Showing the percentage of successful habit completions over time.
	•	Frequency Analysis: Identifying how often specific habits are performed, aiding in recognizing areas needing improvement.

3. Customization and Flexibility

Allowing users to tailor their tracking experience ensures the app meets individual needs. Important aspects include:
	•	Custom Habit Creation: Enabling users to define and track habits specific to their goals.  ￼
	•	Reminders and Notifications: Offering customizable alerts to keep users on track.  ￼

4. Data Export and Integration
Users value the ability to manage and analyze their data externally. Features such as:
	•	Data Export: Allowing users to export their statistics in formats like CSV for personal analysis.  ￼
	•	Third-Party Integration: Syncing with other tools, such as calendars or health apps, to provide a cohesive tracking experience.  ￼

5. Social Sharing
Introducing game-like features can make habit tracking more engaging. Examples include:
	•	Social Sharing: Allowing users to share their progress with friends or within a community for added motivation.  ￼
